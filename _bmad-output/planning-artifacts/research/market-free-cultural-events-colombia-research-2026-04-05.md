## Customer Behavior and Segments

### Customer Behavior Patterns

Colombians in capital cities are **active, high-frequency digital users** who already consume cultural events in large numbers — primarily free ones — but rely on fragmented, non-specialized channels to discover them. Event discovery today is informal: social media feeds, WhatsApp forwards, government portals, and word of mouth. There is no dominant dedicated platform for aggregating free cultural events across cities.

_Behavior Drivers: Social motivation (going with others), cost-sensitivity (free events dramatically outperform paid ones in attendance), identity and local pride, spontaneous planning (same-day or 24–48h advance decisions)._
_Interaction Preferences: Mobile-first, social feed browsing, WhatsApp group sharing, Instagram story tapping._
_Decision Habits: Spontaneous; majority of free event decisions made within 48 hours. Discovery is passive (encountered on feed) more often than active (deliberate search)._
_Source: [DataReportal Digital 2025: Colombia](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-colombia), [Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2024 – Colombia](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2024/colombia)_

### Demographic Segmentation

_Age Demographics: The 18–34 cohort dominates free cultural event attendance. University students (18–25) are the highest-frequency segment for free events. The 35–49 segment attends but less frequently and is more selective._
_Income Levels: Free public cultural events attract broadly across Colombia's socioeconomic strata 2–4 (lower-middle to middle). Paid events skew toward strata 4–6. Cost-free access is a major equalizer and a primary reason attendance at government-organized free events is so high._
_Geographic Distribution: Bogotá has the highest absolute volume of cultural offer and attendance. Medellín has the highest per-capita engagement, sustained by long-term public investment (Parques Biblioteca, Feria de las Flores, Museo de Antioquia). Cali is anchored by music/dance culture (Feria de Cali, salsa scene). Barranquilla peaks around Carnaval._
_Education Levels: Higher education correlates strongly with cultural event attendance. University students and graduates are overrepresented in audiences for visual arts, theatre, classical music, and literary events._
_Source: [DANE Encuesta de Consumo Cultural](https://www.dane.gov.co/index.php/estadisticas-por-tema/cultura/consumo-cultural), [IDARTES Bogotá 2024 data](https://www.bogotanocturna.com/post/en-2025-idartes-se-prepara-para-superar-los-dos-millones-y-medio-de-asistentes-que-llev%C3%B3-en-2024-a-e)_

### Psychographic Profiles

_Values and Beliefs: Strong sense of local/national cultural identity. Pride in Colombian artistic expressions (folklore, cumbia, vallenato, contemporary urban culture). Openness to discovering new formats when cost is zero._
_Lifestyle Preferences: Experiences over material goods — a global Gen Z/Millennial trend that is strong in Colombian urban youth given economic constraints. Weekend leisure oriented around parks, public spaces, and cultural venues._
_Attitudes and Opinions: Positive attitude toward free public culture as a civic right. Government-funded cultural programming (IDARTES, Secretaría de Cultura) is broadly appreciated. Desire for "experiencias auténticas" (authentic local experiences) over commercial entertainment._
_Personality Traits: Sociable and community-oriented. Events are rarely attended alone. Discovery is social — people share what they find via WhatsApp and Instagram._
_Source: [360 Radio – Guía eventos culturales agosto 2024](https://360radio.com.co/guia-de-eventos-culturales-en-bogota-medellin-y-cali-para-agosto-2024-musica-arte-y-diversion/160279/), [Squibb Vicious – Rise of Alternative Leisure Activities, Gen Z](https://squibbvicious.com/2024/03/the-rise-of-alternative-leisure-activities-among-millennials-and-gen-z/)_

### Customer Segment Profiles

_**Segment 1 — The Urban Student (18–25)**_
University student in Bogotá, Medellín, or Cali. Limited budget; actively seeks free activities. Highly mobile (Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp). Discovers events through Instagram posts, Facebook events shared by peers, and university notice boards. Attends concerts al parque, art openings, free theatre, workshops. High frequency, spontaneous planning. **Most valuable segment for Desparchado: high demand, underserved by current discovery tools.**

_**Segment 2 — The Young Professional (25–35)**_
Working, slightly higher income, still cost-conscious. Attends a mix of free and low-cost events. Plans a bit further in advance (3–7 days). Uses Google Search ("qué hacer este fin de semana en Bogotá"), Instagram, and occasionally Eventbrite. Values quality curation over volume — wants to know "what's worth attending." **High value for a well-curated platform like Desparchado.**

_**Segment 3 — The Cultural Family (30–45)**_
Parents looking for quality free activities for children and family. Highly motivated by free entry (economic factor). Discovers via Facebook groups, Alcaldía/city government websites, and word of mouth from other parents. Values safety, accessibility, and family-friendliness in event descriptions. **Underserved by most informal channels; would benefit from Desparchado's structured event data.**

_Source: [Eventbrite Medellín events](https://www.eventbrite.co/d/colombia/medell%C3%ADn-2024/), [Bogotá Agenda Cultural – bogota.gov.co](https://bogota.gov.co/que-hacer/agenda-cultural), [Medellín Alcaldía calendar](https://www.medellin.gov.co/es/eventos/)_

### Behavior Drivers and Influences

_Emotional Drivers: Joy, social bonding, cultural pride, curiosity, and belonging. Free events remove financial anxiety, making attendance a guilt-free decision._
_Rational Drivers: Zero cost is the primary rational driver. Proximity and ease of access (public transport, central locations) are secondary. Clear event descriptions and schedules matter for planning._
_Social Influences: WhatsApp group forwarding is a primary amplification mechanism for free event discovery. Peer recommendations carry more weight than institutional advertising. Instagram Stories from friends attending events drive real-time FOMO and same-day decisions._
_Economic Influences: Colombia's income inequality and stratification system make price sensitivity acute. Free events attract 5–10x the audience of equivalent paid events. The Bogotá Festivales al Parque series (Rock, Jazz, Hip Hop, Salsa) consistently draw hundreds of thousands precisely because they are free._
_Source: [IDARTES 660,000 attendees at Festivales al Parque 2024](https://www.bogotanocturna.com/post/en-2025-idartes-se-prepara-para-superar-los-dos-millones-y-medio-de-asistentes-que-llev%C3%B3-en-2024-a-e), [DataReportal 2025 Colombia](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-colombia)_

### Customer Interaction Patterns

_Research and Discovery: **Passive discovery dominates** — people encounter events while scrolling social media, not by deliberately searching an events platform. Active search ("qué hacer en Bogotá este fin de semana") happens on Google and Instagram. No single aggregator platform dominates. Government portals (bogota.gov.co, medellin.gov.co) list events but have poor UX and limited reach._
_Decision Process: Fast and social. Spot an event → check if friends want to go → decide within hours. Long advance planning is rare for free events. Mobile experience is non-negotiable — desktop is secondary for this audience._
_Post-Event Behavior: High social sharing (Instagram Stories, WhatsApp). Attendees of free events are vocal recommenders. Positive experience at one event increases openness to the organizing institution's future events._
_Loyalty and Retention: Loyalty is to the experience category, not to a discovery channel. Users will return to any platform that reliably surfaces relevant free events in their city. Low switching cost means **curation quality and reliability are the key retention drivers**._
_Source: [Statcounter Social Media Colombia](https://gs.statcounter.com/social-media-stats/all/colombia), [Reuters Digital News Colombia 2024](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2024/colombia), [DataReportal Digital 2025 Colombia](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-colombia)_

## Customer Pain Points and Needs

### Customer Challenges and Frustrations

The central frustration for someone seeking free cultural events in Colombian capital cities is **radical fragmentation**. Events are spread across a patchwork of platforms: city government portals, institutional websites, Facebook Events, Instagram posts, WhatsApp forwards, neighborhood blogs, and print flyers. No single place reliably shows "what free cultural events are happening in my city this week."

_Primary Frustrations:_
- _**Missed events**: By the time a user sees an event on social media, it may be sold out, cancelled, or the same day with no planning time. WhatsApp-forwarded event info dies in chat history — no searchable record._
- _**No free-event filter**: Platforms like Eventbrite and Eventario are oriented toward ticketed events. Free events are not prominently surfaced or tagged. Users must hunt._
- _**City-siloed platforms**: bogota.gov.co only covers Bogotá. medellin.gov.co only covers Medellín. A user in Cali or moving between cities finds no cross-city resource._
- _**Algorithm dependency**: Discovery via Instagram/TikTok is passive and algorithmically filtered — users see what the platform chooses to show, not a comprehensive local events picture._

_Usage Barriers: Government portals exist (IDARTES, bogota.gov.co, medellin.gov.co) but have poor UX and are not designed for spontaneous mobile discovery. They are institutional publishing tools, not audience-facing discovery products._
_Service Pain Points: No platform currently offers event reminders, personalised recommendations by genre/city, or reliable notification of upcoming free events._
_Frequency Analysis: The discovery problem repeats every weekend. The pain is chronic and recurring, not a one-time friction._
_Source: [Why no one has solved event discovery – Hugh Malkin](http://www.hughmalkin.com/blogwriter/2015/9/23/why-no-one-has-solved-event-discovery), [Eventario platform](https://eventario.co/en/), [IDARTES agenda](https://www.idartes.gov.co/es/agenda), [Bogotá cultural agenda](https://bogota.gov.co/que-hacer/agenda-cultural)_

### Unmet Customer Needs

_Critical Unmet Needs:_
- _**A single, reliable, free-event-first aggregator** covering multiple Colombian cities — this does not currently exist at meaningful quality and scale._
- _**Mobile-first, fast experience** for spontaneous "what's on this weekend?" queries. Government portals are not optimised for this._
- _**Clear free/paid distinction** so cost-sensitive users can filter without clicking into each event._
- _**Cross-city coverage** for users who travel between Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, and Barranquilla._
- _**Persistent, shareable event pages** — WhatsApp forwards and Instagram Stories disappear; users need a stable URL to share with friends._

_Solution Gaps: Eventario (Medellín/Bogotá/Cartagena) is the closest existing attempt but does not clearly prioritise free events or cover all capital cities. Government portals cover individual cities but are not designed for discovery. Q Agenda Cultural (Bogotá) exists but had access issues during research, suggesting reliability problems._
_Market Gaps: The "free cultural events across Colombia" niche is genuinely underserved. The closest solutions are either city-locked, paywall-oriented, or institutionally designed rather than audience-first._
_Priority Analysis: The aggregation and free-event-first filtering needs are highest priority — they are foundational. Cross-city and personalisation are secondary._
_Source: [Eventario](https://eventario.co/en/), [Q Agenda Cultural Bogotá](https://www.qagendacultural.com/), [Infolocal Comfenalco Medellín](https://infolocal.comfenalcoantioquia.com/index.php/agenda), [Why event discovery is unsolved](http://www.hughmalkin.com/blogwriter/2015/9/23/why-no-one-has-solved-event-discovery)_

### Barriers to Adoption

_Price Barriers: **None for end users** — Desparchado is free to use. This removes the most common adoption barrier for Colombian audiences who are highly cost-sensitive. However, **22.7% of Colombians still lack internet access** (disproportionately rural and low-income urban), placing a hard ceiling on the addressable audience._
_Technical Barriers: Low for the core urban audience (91.5% of Colombian adults are on social media; mobile internet near-universal in capital cities). Higher for older demographics and rural users, who are outside Desparchado's current target scope._
_Trust Barriers: Government portals have institutional credibility but poor UX. Commercial platforms (Eventbrite) are trusted for ticketed events. A new open-source, non-profit platform will need to build trust through consistent, accurate data before users rely on it as their go-to source._
_Convenience Barriers: The "90% of people search for events only once a week or less" pattern (Malkin, 2015) means low habitual return. Desparchado must either be useful enough in a single session that users bookmark it, or develop a notification/newsletter channel to pull users back without requiring active habit formation._
_Source: [World Bank digital inclusion Colombia](https://blogs.worldbank.org/en/latinamerica/include-everyone-digital-age-colombia), [DataReportal 2025 Colombia](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-colombia), [Event discovery pain points](http://www.hughmalkin.com/blogwriter/2015/9/23/why-no-one-has-solved-event-discovery)_

### Service and Support Pain Points

_Customer Service Issues: Existing platforms (government portals) offer no personalisation, no feedback loop, and no way for users to report outdated or incorrect event listings. Events that are cancelled or rescheduled often remain listed, damaging trust._
_Support Gaps: No existing platform sends reminders or "events this weekend" digests tailored to a user's city and interests. Users must remember to check proactively._
_Communication Issues: Event organisers (museums, theatres, NGOs, community groups) publish to their own channels in isolation. There is no standard format or API for event data, making aggregation technically difficult._
_Response Time Issues: Government portals update on bureaucratic timelines, not real-time. A concert added Tuesday may not appear on bogota.gov.co until Friday._
_Source: [IDARTES agenda](https://www.idartes.gov.co/es/agenda), [AllEvents 2026 roadmap](https://allevents.in/blog/allevents-2026-event-discovery-roadmap/)_

### Customer Satisfaction Gaps

_Expectation Gaps: Users expect "what's free and cultural this weekend near me" to be answerable in under 30 seconds. Current reality: checking 3–5 separate websites/apps, with inconsistent data quality and no unified free-event filter._
_Quality Gaps: Event listings frequently lack key details (exact location, whether registration is required, age restrictions, accessibility info). Government portals prioritise institutional events over the long tail of community and grassroots cultural activities._
_Value Perception Gaps: Users perceive social media as "good enough" for discovery because they don't know what a dedicated, well-curated aggregator would feel like. The gap is invisible until they experience a better alternative._
_Trust and Credibility Gaps: Multiple small blogs and local media outlets (360 Radio, Quira Medios, Vivir en el Poblado) publish cultural agendas, but none is perceived as authoritative or comprehensive._
_Source: [360 Radio cultural agenda](https://360radio.com.co/guia-de-eventos-culturales-en-bogota-medellin-y-cali-para-agosto-2024-musica-arte-y-diversion/160279/), [Quira Medios](https://www.quira-medios.com/agenda-cultural-bogot%C3%A1/)_

### Emotional Impact Assessment

_Frustration Levels: **Moderate but chronic**. Missing a free event that you would have attended is a small but repeated frustration. Over time, users stop trying to find events actively because the effort-to-reward ratio is too low — they default to Netflix/YouTube instead of going out._
_Loyalty Risks: Users have no loyalty to any current discovery channel because none is authoritative. Whoever builds the most reliable, free-event-first experience in Colombian capital cities captures an audience with nowhere else to go._
_Reputation Impact: For Desparchado: the risk is the opposite — **reputation benefit** from being the platform that finally solves this. Cultural institutions and organisers would benefit from a reliable aggregator to reach wider audiences._
_Customer Retention Risks: Without notifications or newsletters, users who find Desparchado once may not return. The "low search frequency" problem means retention requires active outreach, not just a good product._
_Source: [Event discovery unsolved](http://www.hughmalkin.com/blogwriter/2015/9/23/why-no-one-has-solved-event-discovery)_

## Customer Decision Processes and Journey

### Customer Decision-Making Processes

The decision to attend a free cultural event in Colombia is **fast, social, and low-commitment**. Unlike paid events (which involve budget consideration, advance planning, and comparison shopping), free events have a compressed decision cycle — the main friction is awareness, not deliberation. Once someone knows about a relevant free event, the decision to attend is typically made within hours.

_Decision Stages: (1) Passive exposure — encounter event info on social feed or WhatsApp; (2) Quick relevance check — is it free? is it this weekend? does it match my interests?; (3) Social confirmation — forward to friends/partner, check who can come; (4) Attendance or no-show._
_Decision Timelines: Majority of free event attendance decisions are made within 24–48 hours of the event. Weekend planning queries ("qué hacer este fin de semana en Bogotá") peak on Thursday–Friday._
_Complexity Levels: Low complexity for free events — cost is zero, no ticket purchase required, low commitment (easy to cancel). Complexity increases only if the event requires registration or is in an unfamiliar location._
_Evaluation Methods: Rapid visual scan (event poster, photo, description), cost check (free?), location check (easy to get to?), social check (can friends come?)._
_Source: [MeetingHand – Behavioral Economics for Event Planners](https://meetinghand.com/blog/maximizing-attendee-satisfaction-a-behavioral-economy-approach-for-event-planners), [Colombia.com – planes fin de semana](https://www.colombia.com/turismo/noticias/planes-para-el-fin-de-semana-en-bogota-medellin-531368)_

### Decision Factors and Criteria

_Primary Decision Factors (in order of weight):_
1. _**Free admission** — eliminates financial barrier entirely; converts hesitation to "why not?"_
2. _**Relevance to interest** — music genre, art type, activity format must match personal taste_
3. _**Date and time** — must fit the weekend or free time window_
4. _**Location accessibility** — reachable by TransMilenio/Metro or walkable; parking not a primary concern for under-35s_
5. _**Social compatibility** — "can I go with someone?" — very few free cultural events are attended solo_

_Secondary Decision Factors: Weather (outdoor events), safety perception of the venue/neighborhood, event duration, need for prior registration._
_Weighing Analysis: Price is a gate — if it's not free, the other factors barely matter for strata 2–3 audiences. Once the "free" gate is passed, interest relevance is the primary filter._
_Evolution Patterns: Younger users (18–25) are more spontaneous; older users (35–45) plan slightly further in advance and weigh location safety more carefully._
_Source: [GWI – Internet Users Colombia](https://www.gwi.com/blog/internet-users-colombia), [Behavioral Economics in Events](https://meetinghand.com/blog/maximizing-attendee-satisfaction-a-behavioral-economy-approach-for-event-planners)_

### Customer Journey Mapping

_**Awareness Stage** — How users learn about Desparchado and its events:_
- Passive: a friend shares an event link on WhatsApp or Instagram Story
- Active: Google search "qué hacer este fin de semana en Bogotá" → Desparchado appears in results
- Repeat: user bookmarks Desparchado after a positive first visit, returns next weekend
- Social: a tweet/Instagram post from Desparchado's account surfaces in feed

_**Consideration Stage** — Does this event fit me?:_
- Scans event title, photo, date, location — takes 5–10 seconds
- Checks "free?" — if yes, continues; if not, likely bounces
- Reads brief description to confirm relevance
- Checks location on map (is it near a metro/TransMilenio stop?)
- Forwards to WhatsApp group to gauge friends' interest

_**Decision Stage** — Go or don't go?:_
- Decision made after social confirmation ("my friend said yes")
- If solo event (workshop, exhibition), decided more individually based on personal interest intensity
- Decision is almost never about the platform (Desparchado) — it's about the event itself; the platform just needs to get out of the way

_**Attendance Stage** — Getting to the event:_
- Mobile navigation (Google Maps / Waze)
- No ticket to print; free events = just show up
- May share the Desparchado event page link as the "source of truth" when coordinating with friends

_**Post-Attendance Stage** — Sharing and returning:_
- Instagram Stories/Reels from the event
- WhatsApp photos to friends who didn't come → FOMO → next time they join
- May return to Desparchado next weekend if the experience was positive
- No inherent loyalty mechanism — return depends on whether Desparchado surfaces in future "what's on" moments

_Source: [Visit Bogotá events](https://visitbogota.co/es/eventos/agenda-general-148), [Secretaría de Cultura Bogotá – planes imperdibles](https://www.culturarecreacionydeporte.gov.co/es/principal/noticias/estos-son-los-planes-imperdibles-de-la-semana-en-bogota), [OOSGA Colombia social media](https://oosga.com/social-media/col/)_

### Touchpoint Analysis

_Digital Touchpoints:_
- **Instagram**: primary channel where event posters are first encountered (Stories, Reels, Feed posts from cultural institutions)
- **WhatsApp**: primary channel for event forwarding and social confirmation ("going?" / "who's in?")
- **Google Search**: active intent channel — "planes Bogotá este fin de semana", "conciertos gratis Medellín"
- **TikTok**: growing for Gen Z event discovery; event recaps and "come with me" videos drive FOMO
- **Facebook Events**: still relevant for scheduling and RSVPs, especially 25–40 age group
- **Desparchado**: opportunity to be the destination at the Google Search touchpoint and the shareable link in WhatsApp

_Offline Touchpoints: Physical flyers at universities, cafés, cultural centres. Mouth-to-mouth between friends/classmates. Radio (Javeriana Estéreo, UN Radio) for institutional cultural events._
_Information Sources: Social media feeds (primary), WhatsApp groups (primary amplifier), Google Search (active intent), government portals (institutional reference), local media blogs (secondary)._
_Influence Channels: Peer recommendations carry most weight. Institutional announcements (IDARTES, Secretaría de Cultura) have credibility but low viral reach. Influencers and culture journalists have niche but high-trust reach in cultural circles._
_Source: [DataReportal 2025 Colombia](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-colombia), [GWI Colombia internet users](https://www.gwi.com/blog/internet-users-colombia), [OOSGA Colombia](https://oosga.com/social-media/col/)_

### Information Gathering Patterns

_Research Methods: Predominantly **passive scan**, not active search. Users encounter event information while doing other things on their phone (scrolling Instagram, reading WhatsApp messages). Active "event search" behavior is less common but concentrated on Thursdays and Fridays._
_Information Sources Trusted (in order): (1) Friends/peers on WhatsApp; (2) Instagram posts from trusted cultural accounts or institutions; (3) Google search results; (4) Government/institutional websites for details confirmation._
_Research Duration: Very short — typically under 2 minutes from first exposure to decision. The entire evaluation happens on a phone screen in a single session._
_Evaluation Criteria: Free? → Relevant? → This weekend? → Accessible location? → Friends can come? Each question is a gate; failure at any gate ends consideration._
_Source: [GWI – Social media vs. search for research in Colombia](https://www.gwi.com/blog/internet-users-colombia), [Event discovery psychology](https://meetinghand.com/blog/maximizing-attendee-satisfaction-a-behavioral-economy-approach-for-event-planners)_

### Decision Influencers

_Peer Influence: **Dominant**. The "social confirmation" step (forwarding to WhatsApp group) is almost universal for free cultural events. If no friends are available or interested, many users will skip even appealing free events — going alone is a barrier especially for concerts and outdoor festivals._
_Expert Influence: Cultural journalists, radio presenters (Javeriana Estéreo), and Instagram accounts run by cultural curators have high trust in niche audiences (visual arts, jazz, indie music). A mention from a trusted curator converts directly._
_Media Influence: Traditional media (El Tiempo, Caracol) cover major events but not the long tail. Local cultural media (360 Radio, Quira Medios, Vivir en el Poblado) have more specific cultural coverage and loyal niche audiences._
_Social Proof Influence: Attendance numbers and photos from previous editions of recurring events (Rock al Parque, Jazz al Parque) are powerful social proof. An event described as "más de 300,000 asistentes" signals safe, welcoming, worth attending._
_Source: [360 Radio](https://360radio.com.co/guia-de-eventos-culturales-en-bogota-medellin-y-cali-para-agosto-2024-musica-arte-y-diversion/160279/), [Community-driven event participation – ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0264275125008339)_

### Decision Optimizations for Desparchado

These are the friction points Desparchado should specifically design to eliminate:

_Friction Reduction:_
- _Show free/paid status **on the event card** — never make users click to find price_
- _Show **this weekend** and **upcoming** filters prominently — match the Thursday/Friday search intent_
- _Include map/neighborhood tag on every event card — let users evaluate distance in 1 glance_
- _Make events **shareable via WhatsApp** with a single tap — reduce the friction of the social confirmation step_

_Trust Building:_
- _Consistent, up-to-date data is the #1 trust factor — stale or wrong events destroy credibility fast_

_Retention/Return Optimization:_
- _Weekly **"Free events this weekend in [city]"** newsletter directly addresses the low search-frequency problem_
- _City-switching — users who travel between Bogotá and Medellín benefit from a platform that works in both_

_Loyalty Building:_
- _Users return to platforms that **reliably solve the problem** — no loyalty program needed; just consistent, accurate, mobile-first data_
- _Social sharing ("I found this on Desparchado") builds organic word-of-mouth if the event page is the shareable link_

## Competitive Landscape

### Key Market Players

The Colombian cultural events space has no single dominant aggregator. Instead, the landscape is made up of five distinct player types, each serving a partial slice of the market:

**1. Ticketing platforms (paid events only)**
- **TuBoleta** — Colombia's dominant ticketing platform, 27M+ annual website visits, 20+ years in market. Partner of Movistar Arena and TBL-Breakfast Live. Covers concerts, sports, theatre. **Not relevant for free events** — 100% transaction-oriented.
- **Primera Fila / Taquillalive** — Secondary ticketing platforms, significant market presence. Same paid-events focus.
- **Eventbrite** — International platform with Colombia presence. Has a "free events" filter but the platform experience, UX, and content is heavily oriented toward paid/registration-based events.
_Source: [IQ Magazine – Colombia ticketing market](https://www.iq-mag.net/publications/international-ticketing-report/colombia/), [Valora Analitik – TuBoleta datos](https://www.valoraanalitik.com/tuboleta-datos-ticketing-conciertos-eventos-en-colombia/)_

**2. Premium experience platforms**
- **Fever** — International platform (200+ cities) now actively expanding in Colombia (present in Bogotá). Curated, premium cultural experiences: Candlelight concerts, immersive events, thematic dinners. Participated in BIME Bogotá 2025. Strong mobile UX and brand. **Directly competes for the 25–40 premium audience but is positioned toward paid, curated experiences** — not free community events.
_Source: [Correo Cultural – Fever en Colombia](https://correocultural.com/2025/05/fever-tecnologia-cultura-y-experiencia-en-una-sola-plataforma/), [Fever Bogotá](https://feverup.com/en/bogota)_

**3. Local event aggregators**
- **Eventario** — Colombian-built platform covering Medellín, Bogotá, and Cartagena. Cultural, sports, and music events. Closest direct analogue to Desparchado but: no evident free-event priority, no confirmed multi-city free event aggregation, and limited public information about active user base.
- **Infolocal / Comfenalco Antioquia** — Medellín-focused. Broad events calendar (arts, music, sports, ecology). Institutionally backed by Comfenalco (benefit corporation). Good local coverage but geographically locked to Medellín / Valle de Aburrá.

**4. Government / institutional portals**

- **Medellín.gov.co** — City of Medellín events calendar; same institutional limitations.

## Research Synthesis

### Executive Summary

**Desparchado is solving a real problem that nobody else is solving well.**

Colombians in capital cities attend free cultural events at massive scale — IDARTES alone brought over 2 million people to free arts programming in Bogotá in 2024, and the Festivales al Parque series (Rock, Jazz, Hip Hop, Salsa al Parque) drew 660,000+ annually. Demand is not the question. The question this research set out to answer was: does a centralized discovery platform deliver value? The answer is clearly yes.

The current discovery landscape is broken by design: six or more separate platforms (government portals, ticketing sites, editorial blogs, social media feeds) each serve a fraction of the market. None prioritises free events. None covers multiple Colombian cities in a single, searchable, filterable database. The gap Desparchado occupies — free-event-first, multi-city, audience-facing — is real and unoccupied.

The core audience (urban 18–35 Colombians, mobile-first, cost-sensitive, socially-driven) makes event attendance decisions in under 48 hours via WhatsApp social confirmation. They don't lack desire — they lack reliable information. A platform that answers "what's free and worth attending this weekend in my city?" in under 30 seconds on a phone delivers immediate, concrete value.

_Key validation metrics from this research:_
- 2M+ annual attendees at free Bogotá events alone (IDARTES 2024)
- 91.5% of Colombian adults on social media; 8h 44min average daily online time
- No dominant free-event aggregator exists across Colombian capital cities
- Fever, the best-capitalised competitor, is positioned in premium paid experiences — not Desparchado's market

### Strategic Recommendations

**1. Double down on SEO as the primary acquisition channel**

Desparchado's Django architecture with clean URL structure (city/category/event) is inherently SEO-friendly. The queries "eventos gratis Bogotá", "planes gratis Medellín este fin de semana", "qué hacer gratis en Cali" have high search intent and weak competition — editorial articles, not filterable databases. Structured event data (schema.org Event markup) would get Desparchado events surfaced in Google's event rich results.

_Why this matters_: Unlike social media (algorithm-dependent) or paid ads (unsustainable for non-profit), SEO builds a compounding organic audience. A user who finds Desparchado via Google and gets what they need will return and share.
_Source: [SEO for Nonprofits 2025](https://orvador.com/blog/proven-strategies-seo-for-nonprofits-seo-2025/), [Nonprofit SEO WildApricot](https://www.wildapricot.com/blog/nonprofit-seo)_

**2. Solve the retention problem with a weekly newsletter**

The research confirms that 90% of people search for events once a week or less. Waiting for users to return on their own is a losing strategy. A simple weekly "Planes gratis este fin de semana en [ciudad]" email digest — city-personalised, mobile-readable, sent Thursday morning — directly addresses the timing of when users make weekend plans. This is the highest-ROI retention investment available to a small team.

_Source: [Event discovery unsolved](http://www.hughmalkin.com/blogwriter/2015/9/23/why-no-one-has-solved-event-discovery)_

**3. Make WhatsApp sharing frictionless**

The social confirmation step (forwarding event info to friends) is where most free event decisions are made. A prominent "Compartir por WhatsApp" button on every event page, generating a pre-formatted message with event name, date, location, and Desparchado URL, reduces the friction of the single most important action in the user journey.

**4. Pursue institutional data partnerships before audience growth**

Data quality is the foundation of trust. Before scaling audience acquisition, establish data-sharing agreements with IDARTES (Bogotá), Secretaría de Cultura Bogotá, Comfenalco Antioquia/Infolocal (Medellín), and equivalent bodies in Cali and Barranquilla. These institutions publish cultural events as part of their mandate — they have incentive to reach wider audiences. Desparchado offers them distribution; they provide authoritative, fresh data.

_Source: [IDARTES](https://www.idartes.gov.co/es/agenda), [Infolocal Comfenalco](https://infolocal.comfenalcoantioquia.com/index.php/agenda)_

**5. Position for AI discoverability (GEO)**

As AI assistants (ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Perplexity) increasingly answer "what's free this weekend in Bogotá" directly, the platform that provides the most structured, authoritative, and frequently updated event data becomes the source those AIs cite. Publishing an open events API and using schema.org Event markup are the technical steps. Being the authoritative source is the strategic goal.

_Source: [SEO vs GEO for Nonprofits 2025](https://www.elevationweb.org/blog/seo-geo-nonprofits-2025/)_
